As the cold winter holiday season approaches, it’s almost the most wonderful time of the year… unless it’s not. For many of us, we warmly celebrate this festive season with family reunions and sharing of gifts. But out in the community, there are actually people that who do not even have enough clothes to keep warm in this cool weather, let alone gifts or presents.

 

So this winter, transform local lives and share the gift of warmth by donating your second-handed goods to the people in need. See below for the list of items that can or cannot be donated:

 
 

Sew Strong Project


After the collection time ends, the clothing will be contributed to Sew Strong Project by Social Career in which the used clothes will be upcycled into good quality & fashionable wear, packed as a gift and then given to homeless and elderly people during Christmas and New Year.

 

Salvation Army Recycling Programme

 

The remaining collected goods will be given to the Salvation Army Recycling Programme, where the donated items will be refurbished and distributed directly to helping people in need such as home alone elderly, street sleepers, ex-prisoners and CSSA recipients.

 

To learn more about the charities involved and the work they do, please click the buttons below to find out:
